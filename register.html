Standard Guidelines

  • Participant shall NOT refer to any commercial product service unless absolutely required for creative purposes

Technical Guidelines
A. Video Optimisation
  • Shoot horizontally – vertical videos are not allowed
  • Choose your frame carefully. Make sure you don’t have people walking in the background, and the background is aesthetically pleasing. Experiment with a few backgrounds before shooting your performance
    • Place your device such that the camera is at eye level,
    • Frame yourself such that your facial expressions are clear, and your body movements are captured fully.
    • For dancers, stand only as far back from the camera, as you need to be – don’t be so far that the frame is mostly empty space
    • Ensure that your head, hands and torso are not cut by the frame.
    • Look at your device camera while performing and make sure you are framed correctly, preferably centred in the frame
  • Experiment with lighting to get the best video possible and ensure your face is well lit.
    • Make sure the light source isn’t behind you.
    • Dark and silhouette videos will be rejected
  • Try to ensure that your background is a solid colour/texture wall.
  • You need to be dressed such that your clothes contrast the colours of the background, so that you are clearly visible.
  • Please be dressed for the occasion and trade in your jammies or home wear for a more refined wardrobe
  • Hairstyling and makeup is allowed, but make sure your hair doesn’t cover your face
  • Use a tripod/ stable surface if shooting with a phone/DSLR. Avoid handheld shoots
B. Sound Optimisation
  • Conduct a test and ensure your sound input and output by making different permutations and combinations of devices to see what works best for you
  • Choose a silent area to shoot in. Try switching off the fans or other noisy appliances while shooting
  • Tip: For better audio quality, use headphones with microphones
  • Please face your laptop or mobile while speaking, so that the sound gets captured well. It will cut off if you look away from your device while speaking
  • Ensure the words are well enunciated and speak with enough pauses.
  • By default, Zoom edits the audio output to remove background noise, echo and refines the sound. This helps when you are only speaking. But in case if music is being played through a player on your device or via an instrument, these default Zoom settings must be tweaked to get the best output
C. Upload Guidelines

  • The video should be uploaded on your Youtube channel as an unlisted video (the video will not be publicly available and can be watched by only using the shared link)
